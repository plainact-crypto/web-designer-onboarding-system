Why timelines fail

Website timelines fail when dependencies are not clear. A designer may plan design and development time, but forget to account for missing copy, delayed feedback, multiple approvers, domain access issues, or last-minute feature changes.

A good timeline is not just a list of dates. It should show phases, responsibilities, dependencies, review points, and approval deadlines.

Phase 1: Discovery and requirements

Collect the client intake questionnaire.

Confirm business goals and conversion goals.

Define required pages and functionality.

Review competitors or inspiration websites.

Identify content and asset gaps.

Phase 2: Scope and asset collection

Clarify included and excluded work.

Confirm revision rules.

Collect logos, copy, images, testimonials, login access, domain details, and hosting access.

Set a deadline for all missing materials.

Phase 3: Design and feedback

Create the first design direction.

Present the design with context.

Request consolidated feedback by a specific date.

Complete agreed revision rounds.

Confirm design approval before development.

Phase 4: Development, QA, and launch

Build the site.

Test responsiveness, forms, links, performance, SEO basics, analytics, and browser compatibility.

Get client pre-launch approval.

Launch the site.

Recheck the live site after deployment.

Phase 5: Handoff and closure

Send login details and training notes.

Confirm backups and maintenance options.

Send final invoice or payment reminder if needed.

Request a testimonial.

Archive project files and remove access if maintenance is not ongoing.
