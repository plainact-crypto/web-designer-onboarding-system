Target query: what to ask a client before starting a website project

The goal of pre-project questions

Good pre-project questions are not just admin. They protect the project from unclear requirements, late content, unrealistic timelines, and scope creep.

The goal is to understand what the client wants, what the business needs, what is actually ready, and what could block the project later.

Business and goal questions

  • What does your business do?
  • Who are your main customers?
  • What is the main purpose of the website?
  • What action should visitors take: contact, book, buy, subscribe, download, or request a quote?
  • How will you know the website is successful?

Scope and content questions

  • Which pages are required for launch?
  • Which pages are optional or future additions?
  • Who is writing the website copy?
  • Is the copy final, drafted, or missing?
  • Do you have brand assets, photos, testimonials, product descriptions, or service descriptions ready?

Technical and access questions

  • Do you already own the domain?
  • Who manages hosting and DNS?
  • What platform is the current website on?
  • Do you need forms, booking, ecommerce, membership, newsletter signup, CRM integration, or analytics?
  • Who will maintain the website after launch?

Decision and approval questions

  • Who is the main decision maker?
  • Who else needs to approve design or content?
  • How quickly can feedback be provided?
  • Are there any fixed deadlines or launch events?
  • What could delay the project?
