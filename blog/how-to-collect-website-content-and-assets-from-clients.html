Target query: how to collect website content and assets from clients

Why asset collection matters

Web design projects often stall because content and assets arrive late, incomplete, or scattered across emails, messages, and old folders.

A clear asset collection process helps the client understand what is needed and helps the designer avoid constant chasing.

Brand assets to request

Logo files in SVG, AI, EPS, PNG, or JPG formats.

Brand colors and hex codes.

Font names and licensing details.

Brand guidelines if available.

Approved icons, illustrations, or graphic elements.

Content to request

Final copy for each page.

Product or service descriptions.

Team bios and headshots.

Testimonials, reviews, awards, and case studies.

FAQs, pricing details, contact information, and calls to action.

Technical access to request

Current website admin login.

Domain registrar access.

Hosting control panel access.

DNS access if separate.

Analytics, Search Console, email marketing, CRM, and integration access.

How to follow up without sounding aggressive

Send a polite missing-assets email with a short list of what is outstanding.

Explain which project stage depends on those materials.

Give a clear deadline.

Explain that the timeline may shift if materials are delayed.

Keep the tone professional and practical.
