Target query: web designer client onboarding checklist

Phase 1: Qualify the lead

  • Confirm the business type and website goal.
  • Check whether the budget fits your normal project range.
  • Confirm the decision maker and approval process.
  • Ask about timeline, deadlines, and launch pressure.
  • Flag red risks such as vague scope, missing content, or too many approvers.

Phase 2: Collect requirements

  • Send a website requirements questionnaire.
  • Ask for target audience, conversion goal, required pages, and features.
  • Collect reference websites and brand direction.
  • Clarify content ownership and responsibility.
  • Document integrations, forms, hosting, domain, and maintenance expectations.

Phase 3: Clarify scope

  • List included pages, templates, and features.
  • List excluded work clearly.
  • Define revision rounds and change request rules.
  • Confirm client responsibilities and approval points.
  • Document open assumptions before kickoff.

Phase 4: Collect assets

  • Request logos, brand colors, fonts, copy, images, testimonials, and login access.
  • Set deadlines for missing materials.
  • Confirm domain, hosting, CMS, analytics, and integration access.
  • Use a follow-up email if assets are late.

Phase 5: Kickoff, launch, and handoff

  • Run a structured kickoff call.
  • Confirm timeline, roles, risks, and next steps.
  • Use pre-launch QA before going live.
  • Get client approval before launch.
  • Deliver handoff notes, training, backups, and maintenance options.

FAQ

What is the most important onboarding step?

Scope clarity is usually the most important step because unclear scope causes revision loops, delays, and uncomfortable client conversations.

Should I send every checklist to every client?

No. Use the parts that match the project size. Smaller projects may only need intake, scope, assets, and launch checks.
