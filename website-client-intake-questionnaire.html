Target query: website client intake questionnaire

Business and project basics

  • What does your business do?
  • Who are your main customers?
  • What is the main purpose of the new website?
  • Do you currently have a website? If yes, what platform is it on?
  • What is the desired launch timeline?

Website goals and pages

  • What is the primary action visitors should take?
  • Which pages are required?
  • Which pages are optional or future additions?
  • Do you need a blog, portfolio, booking flow, shop, or member area?
  • How will success be measured after launch?

Content and brand readiness

  • Who will provide the website copy?
  • Is the copy final, drafted, or missing?
  • Do you have logos, brand colors, fonts, and image assets?
  • Do you have testimonials, case studies, product descriptions, or team bios?
  • Are legal pages available, such as privacy policy and terms?

Functionality and technical needs

  • Do you need forms, newsletter signup, booking, payments, maps, chat, or CRM integration?
  • Do you already own the domain?
  • Who manages hosting and DNS?
  • Do you have analytics or Search Console access?
  • Do you need post-launch maintenance?

FAQ

When should I send the intake questionnaire?

Send it after the lead looks qualified but before the kickoff call or proposal finalization.

Should the questionnaire be long?

It should be detailed enough to avoid confusion, but grouped clearly so clients can complete it without feeling overwhelmed.

What if the client cannot answer?

That is useful information. It may show that discovery, content planning, or scope clarification is needed before design starts.
