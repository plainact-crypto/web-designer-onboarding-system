Preview the workflow

This preview shows the logic of the Web Designer Client Onboarding System without giving away the full paid asset. The full PDF is designed to help a freelance web designer move from a vague inquiry to a clear project brief, defined scope, organized assets, kickoff plan, launch process, and handoff.

1. Lead Triage

Identify fit, budget, timeline, decision maker, risks, and project type before investing too much time.

2. Requirements

Collect goals, pages, functionality, content readiness, brand direction, integrations, and maintenance expectations.

3. Scope

Define included work, excluded work, revision rules, client responsibilities, assumptions, and change request boundaries.

4. Assets

Request logos, copy, images, domain, hosting, CMS access, analytics, and third-party platform details.

5. Kickoff

Run a structured call that confirms the sitemap, content status, roles, risks, timeline, and next steps.

6. Launch & Handoff

Use pre-launch checks, final approvals, handoff notes, training details, maintenance options, and closure steps.

Common problem this solves

Without a system, designers often chase missing content, accept vague scope, manage scattered feedback, and lose time before the real work begins.
